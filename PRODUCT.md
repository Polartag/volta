# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Decision-makers at nonprofit and social-sector organizations (Geschäftsführung, Bereichsleitungen, Verwaltungsleiter) evaluating a custom software offer. They read carefully, compare positions, and want to trust the vendor. Context: a formal written proposal they may print or share internally.

## Product Purpose

volta.codes is a Berlin-based custom software and AI consultancy for the German market. This document is a formal Angebot (proposal) — a written commitment from the company that must communicate competence, reliability, and partnership on equal terms. Success means the reader feels confident enough to sign.

## Brand Personality

Präzise, ruhig, kompetent. Sober and direct — no marketing fluff, no superlatives, no padding. The voice is confident without being loud.

## Anti-references

- Generic Word/LibreOffice proposals: Times New Roman, boilerplate header, serial-letter aesthetic
- Agency brochures: big hero images, excessive color, oversized brand marks
- Stiff legal documents: dense justified body text, no visual hierarchy, institutional coldness
- SaaS landing pages repurposed as documents: metrics callouts, gradient accents, badge grids

## Design Principles

1. **Typography carries the weight.** No decorative elements — hierarchy comes from size, weight, and whitespace alone.
2. **The document earns trust through clarity.** Every section should be instantly scannable; the reader should never lose their place.
3. **Brand identity, not brand advertising.** The volta.codes blue appears purposefully, not decoratively.
4. **Structure makes the content.** Tables, pricing blocks, and the timeline are designed as information objects, not afterthoughts.
5. **Print-aware from the start.** The layout must work on screen and as a printed/exported PDF.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. The document will be read by a broad range of users; avoid relying on color alone to convey meaning. Tables need proper headers. Print stylesheet should be considered.
